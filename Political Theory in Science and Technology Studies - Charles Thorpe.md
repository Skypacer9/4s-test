* Kuhn's reincarnation of Kierkegaard, Feyerabend's revival of Emerson, Polanyi's version of Burke - questions of political theory have been important, but encoded an implicit, within the fields of philosophy, history, and sociology of science throughout their 20th century development

		* Polyani' in opposition to Popper and Merton's equating of science with skepticism, Polyani argued both science and liberal democracy depended on trust and authority - Polyani insisted that science was rooted in faith and that the scientific community was a community of believers rather than skeptics - skepticism was corrosive of the sense of social belonging and tradition that maintained scientific authority and liberal democratic political order - he argued skeptical and materialist modern philosophers had resulted in totalitarianism. The preservation of both science and democracy meant maintaining a tradition, the most important elements of which were tacit and taken on faith - not only liberal but also 'profoundly conservative'
