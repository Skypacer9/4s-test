SO430: Economic Sociology

Sociologists have always studied economic processes and the roots of economic sociology extend back to the classical social theory of Marx, Weber, Du Bois, Perkins Gilman, Durkheim, and Simmel. But the last few decades have seen a flowering of scholarship in this area. Are we really rational utility-maximisers? What is ‘the economy’ and what is its relationship to society? How does economic life reflect and enact moral categories? How can we understand the (re)production of economic ‘winners’ and ‘losers’? In the first half of the term, this course offers a general introduction to the theoretical foundations of economic sociology. The second half of the term puts particular emphasis on the rise of the digital economy. This structure provides an opportunity to engage with the above questions through both sociologically grounded accounts of economic phenomena and sociological critiques of the analytical assumptions and research procedures common in mainstream economics.
