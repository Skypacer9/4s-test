* The commodity form as developed by Marx is defined by the way in which it realizes value as a value form through exchange
* Abstraction, quantification, transferability are central for generating exchange value. Commodities are linked to a view of the market as the key economizing arena - it dissolves social obligations and imposes a tangible abstraction value on social relations of production
* The asset is presumed to be 'not for sale' and does not realize its value in the moment of exchange, neither is it necessarily produced
	* It rests on 'rent and enclosure without a particular orientation toward sale'
	* Referring to the International Accounting Standards Board for a pragmatic and technical definition of asset: 'a resource controlled by the entity as a result of past events and from which future economic benefits are expected to flow to the entity'

* The juxtaposition of commodity and asset should not be miscontrued as a historical argument to replace one economic form with another - it does not cease to exist but remains a part of ensuring the liquidity and transferability that continues to be crucial for processes of capitalization 
* The asset does not replace the commodity - it challenges the understanding of temporality and value associated with the commodity form - temporarily of the commodity is linked to phases of production that represent the sunk cost of the past, as well as uncertainty and volatility of market exchange in the (future) present: a past of expenditure and a future moment of the realization of value.
* Assets bring to the fore a different economic sense - an institutionalization and ongoing presence of a property title that affords a claim on value - linked to collaterized, contracted and de-risked future that is governed to make the claim on value hold true
* The asset has an intense orientation towards the future and its novel modes of evaluation, the asset appears to be almost interchangeable with a particular understanding of financialized capital - it is defined by its circular logic of profitable return
