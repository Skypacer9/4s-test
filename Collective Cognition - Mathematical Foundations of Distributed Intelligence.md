* Lots of individual cognition is enhanced by communication and collaboration with other intelligent agents -> collective cognition, by analogy with the well known concept of collective action
* People often think better in groups in ways that might be impossible for isolated individuals
	* -> modern science
	* -> hayek argued this was competitive markets
	* The study of collective cognition has been qualitative, philosophical and often anecdotal - recently there have been tools to initiate a rigorous quantitative science of collective cognition
* It involves an interaction among three eleemnts - the individual abilities of agents, shared knowledge, communication structure
* They resemble many other complex systems - collectives of goal directed processes - the individual processes know little of the detailed dynamics and state of the overall system and have to use ad aptive techniques to achieve their goals
	* many engineered systems of the future have to be of this type, with massively distributed computational elements
	* Multi-agent system (MAS) - there is optimism in this field that widely applicable solutions to large, distribute problems are close at hand (lol, this was written in the early 2000s)
	* some believe that in information and telecommunication networks of today we have nascent examples of artificial cognitive collectives
	* Much scientific work has been done on the forward problem of deducing global behaviour that ensues for various choices of design parameters for collectives - the forward problem concerns evolution over time of shared knowledge of agents
		* how analogous is this to organic evolution (knowledge evolution)
	* Starting with a desired global behaviour and deisgning the goals of constituent agents to produce that behaviour -> mechanism design - but this is limited by human psychology (not necessarily shared by simpler/less intelligent agents or those with different degrees of freedom)
* We need to know how to make a large collective work toward a specific goal in a decentralized, adaptive way
	* 1.agents need to work collectively so that as a group, their behavior solves the overall problem
	* 2. both the agents and their collaborative structure need to be adaptive
* first property is crucial to modular deisgn for large or inherently distributed problems (like satellite constellations, planetary exploration rovers)
* second property - lies in how agents interact with one another and the environment - both the environment and responses of other agents to changes in the environment will modify the background state one agent perceives before choosing its actions 
* Only a rigorous, quanitative science of collective cognition can provide us with broadly applicable methods of engineering the massive computational systems of the future - also science would provide new tools for investigating naturally occurring collectives including the ones in which we participate
