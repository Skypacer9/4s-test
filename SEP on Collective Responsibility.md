* The notion of collective responsibility, like personal responsibility and shared responsibility refers in most contexts to both the causal responsibility of moral agents for harm in the world and blameworthiness that we ascribe to them for having caused such harm.
* Moral vs causal responsibility
* Both are associated with groups rather than in just individual 'free will'
* Methodological individualists challenge the possibility of associating moral agency with groups vs individual members. Normative individualists argue that collective responsibility violates principles of both individual responsibility and fairness. In response proponents of collective responsibility set out to show collective responsibility as well as group intentions, collective action, and group blameworthiness, are metaphysically possible and can be ascribed to agents fairly in at least some if not all cases. 
* Some three further concerns have been raised. 
	* 1. Whether groups meet the same stringent conditions of moral responsibility that individuals do
	* 2. Advantages and disadvantages of holding particular kinds of groups 
	3. Moral implications for group members from moral responsibility of their group for harm (do they become morally responsibility for harms and how is blame distributed)
* There has been more discussion of forward looking collective resopnsibility recently -> it doesn't focus on whether a particular collective agnet caused harm or involves itself with blame in general - it focuses on what if anything the agent can be expected to do with respect to remedying the harm and preventing its reoccurence -> remedial responsibility

6. Collective Responsibility and the Question of Consequences
* When is it appropriate to hold a group for harm - when is it appropriate to refrain from holding a group responsible? As things now stand, we generally assume that to hold a group or for that matter an individual responsible for harm is to simply establish that they are responsible for the harm
* To hold an agent responsible for the harm is to make the agents' responsibility known to both them and to the rest of the community - to publicize their responsibility as part of a social or legal practice of accountability in particular contexts with particular purposes in mind
* Our holding X responsible is a amtter of what we do with our knowledge of X
* When are we justified in performing an act of accountability - since holding agents responsible for harm sheds a engative light on them and frequently calls for compensation/punishment, we take fairness seriously and require accuracy with respect to the facts of responsibility
* Fairness is not always just a matter of factual accuracy when it comes to holding groups responsible - it can be and often is -a matter of making sure we do not in our holding of agents responsible discirminate between equally responsible agents - treating like cases in the same fashion so as to not be discriminatory - making sure that if collective responsibility is ascribed to particular  groups it is ascribed to all groups according to general rules
	* sometimes we discriminate between these cases on consequentialist grounds
	* in many of these cases we are concerned with whether or not we can bring about positive consequences in the world by holding particular groups responsible - would holding particular groups responible for harm lead to greater animosity among groups? create resentment in the community, stand in the way of peace?
* Those concerned about responsibility in philosophical circles often hestiate to enter a full blown consequentialist debate about when we should hold particular agents responsible in practice
	* In the case of individual responsibility, these consequences tend to be positive and include the reinforcement of norms associated with moral agency
	* In the case of collective responsibility these tend to be positive and negative
* We can do important things in the world by holding particular groups responsible for harm - we can raise consciousness among groups about what they are doing, get them to stop harming others, reinforce social norms that prevent such harm from occurring in the future, make clear to the world that those being harmed are worth taking seriously
* Holding groups responsible can provide the basis for institutional reform in cases where collective acts were hitherto invisible
