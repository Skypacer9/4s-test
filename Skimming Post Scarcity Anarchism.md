Introduction to the First Edition

* Human society developed around the brute issues posed by unavoidable material scarcity and their subjective coutnerpart in denial, renunciation, and guilt
* historical splits - man from nature and man from man - origins in survival
* material scarcity provided the historic rational for the development of patriarchal family, private property, class domination, the state (really?) - or was it also psychological scarcity or desire? - it nourished great divisions in hiearchical society that pitted town against country, mind against sensuousness, work against play, individual against society, the individual against hismelf
* Whether the long development could have followed a benign course is irrelevant - it is behind us (I don't think it's irrelevant, it says something about the worth of different strategies and ways of being) - did hiearchical society have to complete its bloody journey?
* We have created many new machines and resources which can provide food, shleter, garments, and a broad spectrum of luxuries without devouring the precious time of humanity and without dissipating its invaluable reservoir of creative energy in mindless labour - post-scarcity society
* In no way has existing society realized the post-scarcity threshold potential of its technology - neither material privileges that modern capitalism affords the middle class nor its wasting of resources reflect the rational, humanistic, unalienated content of a post-scarcity society
* Post scarcity isn't jsut material, it refers the social and cultural apparatus that foster insecurity in the psyche - this could be oppressive limits established in the natural world or a class s tructure  - human relationships and psyche of the individual in the post-scarcity society reflect freedom, security, and self expression that this abundance makes possible - post scarcity society is the fulfilment of the social and cultural potentialities latent in a technology of abundance
* The system tends to degrade the middle classes - turns them into a commodity and complicity in own oppression, assimilating wants to the commodity nexus, the petty bouregois vibrates with insecurity - abundance could be used for human ends - a technology of abundance that can provide the material basis for liberation??
* The greatest strength of capitalism lies in its ability to subvert revolutionary goals by the ideology of domination - capitalism is the heir of history, legatee of repressive features of earlier hierarchical societies, bourgeois ideology has been pieced together from older elements of social domination and questioning - we often mistake these for human nature 
* Hierarchy, sexism, renunciation don't disappear with democratic centralism etc - what needs to wither away is not just the state but the consciousness of domination
* ==Planned economy - already achieved by capitalism - capacity of state capitalism to assimilate large areas of Marxist doctrine as official ideology, in advanced capitalist countries the progress of technology has removed one of the most important reasons for the existence of the "socialist state" - "to increase the total of productive forces as rapidly as possible"-> we shouldn't loiter around the issues of a "planned economy"== and a "socialist state - issues created by an earlier stage of capitalism and by a lower stage of technological development. The revolutionary project must become commensurate with the enormous social possibilities of our time - vision of freedom. The social dialectic begins to mature, in terms of what must be abolished and what must be created.

	* What we must create to replace bourgeois society is not only the classless society envisioned by socialism but the nonrepressive utopia envisioned by anarchism
	* Until now - occupied primarily with technological capabilities of bourgeois society, potential for supporting a post-scarcity society and tension created between what is and what could be -> people begin to find intolerable the social, economic, and cultural conditions accepted only a decade or so ago
		* In the 60s and 70s there were an explosion of liberation movements: black liberation, women's liberation, youth liberation, children's liberation, gay liberation - privileges of yesterday are becoming the rights of today 
		* The very concept of "rights" is becoming suspect as expression of a patronizing elite which bestows and denies "rights" and "privileges" to niferiors. A struggle against elitism and hierarchy as such is replacing the struggle for "rights" as the main goal - it is not justice that is demanded but freedom
	* What gods "exepctations" into "rising" is the ir rationality of the capitalist system - when automation can reduce toil to the near vanishing point, nothing is more meaningless than a lifetime of toil. ==When modern industry can provide abundance for all, nothing is more vicious to poor people than a lifetime of poverty.== (proof, and what are the structures to organize the uplift when there are tremendous disparities in knowhow or even health?) When all resources exist to promote social equality (what is this?) nothin is more criminal to ethnic minorities, women, homosexuals than subjugation.
	* Capitalism emerges as the "other" of humanity's deepest desires and impulses 
	* Subcultures emerge which emphasize a natural diet, extended family, sexual freedom, tribalism, community, mutual aid, communism, anarchism -> by refusing to live by bourgeois structures, the first seeds of the utopian lifestyle are planted  -> dropping into the tentative, experimental, ambiguous social relations of Utopia.  The lifestyle is indispensable in preserving the integrity of the revolutionary in provindg them with the psychic resources to resist the subversion of the revolutionary project by bourgeois values. 
* Any attempt to solve the environmental crisis within a bourgeois framework must be dismissed as chimerical - capitalism is inherently anti-ecological - competition and accumulation constitute its very law of life - "production for the sake of production" - anything "has its price" and is fair game for the marketplace - nature is necessarily treated as a mere resource to be plundered and exploited  - this is not the result of hubristic blunders but from the logic of capitalist production
* The schizoid attitude of the public towards technology - fear and hope - should not be dismissed lightmindedly - dualism implied by tech - same faces of a common social dialectic - if there was any truth to Marx's claim that hierarchical society was "historically necessary" in order to "dominate" nature -> "dominating" nature emerged from the domination of man by man

* An anarchist or anarcho-communist society presupposes the abolition of private property, the distribution of goods according to individual needs, the dissolution of commodity relationships, the rotation of work, a decisive reduction in the time devoted to labour -> this is the anatomy but not the physiology of freedom
	* -> anarchists have given more attention to the subjective problem of revolution than any other revolutionary movement -> a libidinal upsurge of the people, a stirring of the social unconscious that reaches back to the earliest struggles of humanity against domination and authority - human beings an end in themselves and life a revered, marvelous experience - these ideologies have often reduced human beings to means -"the People", "Freedom"
	* Differences between socialists and anarchists reveal themselves in teh conflicting types of organization and praxis
		* Socialists organize into hierarchical bodies
		* Anarchists base their organizational structures on the "affinity group" - a collective of intimate friends who are no less concerned with their human relationships than their social goals - the very mode of anarchist organization transcends the traditional split between psyche and social world - if the need arises, there is nothign to prevent affinity groups from coordinating into large movements (the Spanish anarchists built a nationwide federation of thousands out of this nuclear form) - movements have the advantage that control over the larger organization lies with the affinity groups vs coordinating bodies -> all action is based on voluntarism and self discipline not coercion and command - praxis is liberatory in the personal as well as social arena
	* Anarchist conception of "direct action" - regarded as a tactic, a method of abolishing the state without recourse to state institutions and techniques -> it doesn't go far enough - it's a basic revolutinoary strategy, a mode of praxis intended to promote the individuation of the "masses" - it's function is to assert the identity of the particular within the framework of the general - more  important than its political implications are its psychological effects - direct action makes people aware of themselves as individuals who can affect their own destiny
	* Also emphasizes spontaneity - inner process - it can exist on many levels, and be more or less permeated by knowledge, insight, experience - belief in spontaneous development - every development must be free to find its own equilibrium
		* spontaneity in social life converges with spontaneity in nature to provide the basis for an ecological society - the ecological principles that shaped organic societies reemerge in the form of social principles to shape Utopia
		* Whether now or in the future human relationships with nature are always mediated by science, technology, and knowledge - but whether these will improve nature to its own benefit will depend upon man's abilitty to improve their social condition - either revolution will create an ecological society, wih new ecotechnologies and ecocommunities, or humanity and the natural world as we know it today will perish
* Every revolutionary epoch is a peroid of convergence when separate processes collect to form a socially explosive crisis - earlier revolutionary epochs dealt with familiar institutional categories - family, religion, property, toil and the state
* But today there is no future, utopia and social extinction- learning to live is the only mode of survival, play the only mode of work, personal the only mode of the social, the abolition of sex roles the only mode of sexuality, tribalism the only mode of family, sensuality the only mode of rationality.
* These also converge on a specific locus in time and space where the social crisis is most acute - in the 17th century this was in ENgland, in the 18th and 19th century, France, in the early 20th, Russia - in the late 20th century this is the US - centre of world counterrevolution - to ignore the strategic position of the US both historically and internationally would reveal an incredibly insensitivity to reality
* The decay of the American institutional structure results not from mystical "failure of nerve" or imperialist adventures in the Third World but from the overripeness of America's technological potential
* A great deal of the pursuit can be diverted into established institutional channels for a time - but the social crisis is too deep and world-historical for established institutions to contain it - if the system failed to assimilate the black movement, the student movement of the sixties, it was not for want of institutional flexiblity and resources  - these movements rejected what established institutions had to offer - their demands increased as each one was met - the physical base of movements expanded - black, hippie and student radicalism percolated through the country, penetrating high schools as well as universities, suburbs and ghettoes, rural communities as well as cities.
* The contradiction between scarcity and the potential for post-scarcity apepars in the contradiction - young people of the 60s are disenchanted by a consumerism that pacifies but never disatisfies - if young people reject consuermism, work ethic, hierarchy, and authority, they are more "proletarian" than proletariat 
* American revolutionaries have yet to find a voice that relates to American issues - First World problems are not Third World problems - the two are not bridged by retreating to ideologies that deal with nineteenth century problems 
	* What the Third World needs is a revolution in America
	* We need a cohesive, revolutionary approach to American social problems 
* The problems of scarcity, from which emerged propertied forms, calsses, the state, all the cultural paraphernalia of domination, can now be resovled by a post-scarcity society 
* Man's desire for unrepressed spontaneous expression, for variety in experience and surroundings, an environment scaled to human dimensions must also be realized to achieve natural equilibrium -> exploitation, racism, poverty, class struggle, imperialism are still with us - these can never fade from revolutionary theory and praxis until they are resovled completely
* The traditional workers' movement will never reappear - "bread and butter" issues are too well contained by bourgeois unionism to form the basis for the old socialist type of labour union - but workers may form radical organizations to fight for changes in the quality of their lives and work - ==for workers' management of production== - only when life issues dominate factory issues will factory issues be assimialted to lfie issues

Post Scarcity Anarchism

Preconditions and Possibilities
* Revolutions of the past have been particularistic revolutions of minority classes -> ideas of "nation", "free citizen", "equality before the law" - concealed the mundane reality of the cnetralized state, atomized individual, dominance of bourgegois interest  - these revolutions replaced the rule of one class by another, one system of exploitation by another, one system of toil by another, and one system of psychological repression by another
* The possibility of the general revolution -> the technological revolution has created the objective, quantitative basis for a world without class rule, exploitation, toil or material want 
* How can people be free in the concrete as well as abstract - they have to be free from material want, from toil, from devoting the greater part of their time to struggle with necessity - emphasizing that freedmo supposes free time and the material abundance for abolishing free time as a social privilege - great contribution of Marx to modern revolutionary theory 
* The preconditions is not the conditions fore freedom - the bourgeois control of technology reinforces established organization of society and every day life -  technology and the resources of abundance furnish capitalism with the means for assimialting large sections of society to established systems of hierarchy and authority 
* The attempt of bourgeois society to control and exploit the environment has devastating consequences -> pollution and destruction of the ecology required for a complex organism like humans - radiation, toxic waste, worldwide contamination (this is a bit outdated )
* Thermal pollution and Co2 pollution

The Redemptive Dialectic
* Does a dialectic guide the social development in the direction of an anarchic society where people will gain full control over their daily lives - or does the social dialectic come to an end with capitalism, its possibilities sealed off by the use of a highly advanced technology for repressive and cooptative purposes 
* Limits of Marxism - in a period of material scarcity, anchored the social dialectic in the economic realm - he examine the preconditions for liberation not conditions of liberation - its humanistic theory of alienation turns primarily on the issue of work and man's alienation from the product of his labour 
* Today capitalism is a parasite of the future, a vampire that survives on the technology and resources of freedom
* Industrial capitalism of Marx's time organized its commodity relations around a system of material scarcity - state capitalism organized its commodity relations around a prevailing system of material abundance - today scarcity has to be enforced -> the social dialectic and contradictions of capitalism have expanded from the economic to hiearchical realms of society (how true is this??)
	* The dialectic of bureaucratic state capitalism originates in the contradiction between the repressive character of commodity society and the enormous potential freedom opened by technological advance - the contradiction opposes the exploitative organization of society to the natural world - this includes not only the environment but also humanity's nature - Eros derived impulses  - this contradiction is beyond coopting - physical nature is not redeemable by reforms, concessions, modification of policy 
	* This contradiction also manifests itself as the banalization and impoverishment of experience in a bureaucrtically manipulated impersonal mass society - Eros derived impulses can never really be eliminated  - these are renewed with birth and youth 
	* Contradictions within bureaucratic state capitalism permeate all the hierarchical forms developed and overdeveloped by bourgeois society - it's not a question to ask whether they were ever "progressive" in the Marxian sense - the problems of necessity and survival have become congruent with those of freedom and life - there is no longer theoretical mediation, "transitional stages", centralized organizations to bridge the gap between the existing and possible  - problems of transition which occupied Marxists for nearly a century are eliminated not only by the advance of technology but by the social dialectic 
		* The absolute negation of the state is anarchism. The absolute negation of the city is community - community in which the social environment is decentralized into rounded, ecologically balanced communes. The absolute negation of bureaucracy is direct relations in a general free assembly of individuals (but how can you organize this so it doesn't get chaotic?). The absolute negation of the centralized economy is regional ecotechnology. The absolute negation of the patriarchal family is liberated sexuality - in which all forms of sexual regulation are transcended by spontaneous, untrammeled expression of eroticism among equals. The absolute negation of the marketplace is communism - in which collective abundance and cooperation transform labor into play and need into desire.

Spontaneity and utopia
* A rediscovery. Ecology has shown that balance in nature is achieved by organic variation and complexity not homogeneity and simplification - biodiversity stabilizes populations of pests. Left to itself, ecosystems tend towards organic differentiation, greater variety and diversity in the number of prey and predator. That doesn't mean no interference - the need for productive agriculture always has to be foregrounded in an ecological approach to food cultivation and forest management - man can often produce changes in an ecosystem that would improve ecological quality - but these efforts require insight and understanding.
	* Food cultivation has to be through cooperation rather than factory farming or monoculture. The agriculturalist has to be familiar with ecology and aware of its possibilities. Same applies to pollution control - small industrial units and diversifying energy sources. 
* The implications of small scale agriculture and industry for a community are obvious - if humanity is to use the principles needed to manage an ecosystem - the basic communal unit of social life has to become an ecosystem - an ecocommunity - it has to be diversified, balanced and well-rounded 
* The goal has to be liberation of daily life - the self must be identifiable - it has to be self liberation that reaches social dimensions, not mass liberation behind which lurks the rule of an elite- if t he self is not changed, it will once again circumvent those whose lives are to be lived every day and leave daily life unaffected 
* There can be no separation of the revolutionary process from the revolutionary goal - a society whose fundamental aim is the self administration in all facets of life that can only be achieved by self activity
* Revolution as self activity is the paramount feature of all the great revolutions in modern history - the journey of the sans culottes in 1792 and 1793, the famous Five Days of February in 1917 in petrgrad, the uprising of the Barcelona proletariat in 1936, the early days of the Hungarian Revolution in 1957, the May-June evenets in Paris in 1968 - these uprisings have been initiated by the self activited of the masses - in flat defiance of teh hesitant policies advanced by revolutionary organizations -> these turned life into a festival
* The puritanism and work ethic of the traditional left stem from a force opposing revolution - the capacity of the bourgeois environment to infiltrate the revolutionary framework - the origins of this power lie in the commodity nature of man under capitalism - quality automatically transferred to the organized group and which the group reinforces in its members
* The problem of alienation can never be completely resolved apart from the revolutionary process but it can be guarded agaanist - this is only when the revolutionary group recognizes it is a catalyst in the revolutionary process, not a 'vanguard' - its goal is not the seizure of power but the dissolution of power  - the problem of power, of control from below or above - can only be solved if there is no above or below
* The group must remain open to public scrutiny not only in formulated decisions but in the very formulation - practice and theory has to be united. Commodity relations have to be done away with.

Prospect

* De institutionalization in bourgeois social structure. Millions of people are shedding their commitment to the society in which they live - they no longer believe its claims or respect its symbols and goals and they almost intuitively refuse to live by institutional and social codes*
* This comes from an opposition to war to a hatred of political manipulation in all its forms - it started from a rejection of racism and detestation of middle-class values and lifestyles - to a rejection of the commodity system. From an irritation with environmental pollution, it passes into a rejection of the American city and modern urbanism. 
* The period in which we live resembles the revolutionary Enlightenment that swept through France in the 18th century - a period that completely reworked French consciousness and prepared the conditions for the Great Revolution of 1789. Old institutions were slowly pulverized b y molecular action from below long before they were toppled by mass revolutionary action - there was a growing day to day disobedience. Society became Dioynysian - dramatic increase in the rate of official crimes.  An ever increasing number of people - began to spontaneously engage in their own defiant propaganda of the deed.
	* Contradictions - radical ideology oscillated between rigid scientism and sloppy romanticism. Notions of freedom were anchored in precise, logical ideal of self-control, but also with vague, instinctive norms of spontaneity.
